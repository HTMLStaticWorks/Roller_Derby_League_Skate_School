MASTERING THE POWER JAM

By Coach Velocity | May 10, 2026

A Power Jam occurs when the opposing jammer is sent to the penalty box, leaving your jammer as the only person on the track who can score points. This is the most critical time in a roller derby match and can often determine the outcome of the game.

To maximize a Power Jam, your blockers must shift from a defensive mindset to an offensive one. Clearing a path for your jammer while preventing the opposing blockers from reformed walls is key.

1. CLEAR THE TRACK

Your blockers should identify the weakest point in the opposing wall and use coordinated blocks to open a lane. Communication is vital here; if the jammer sees a hole on the inside line, they need to call it out.

"Speed is important, but control is everything. In a power jam, a calm jammer is a scoring jammer."

2. RECYCLE THE BLOCKERS

When you knock an opponent out of bounds, your blockers should "recycle" them by skating backward to force the opponent to re-enter the track behind you. This buys your jammer more time and space to complete laps.
